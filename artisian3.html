COCONUT BUTTER QUESTIONS

WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN COCONUT BUTTER AND
COCONUT OIL?

Coconut Butter is the pureed meat of mature coconuts. It is roughly 60% oil, but it also has the fiber,
naturally-occurring sugars, and other nutrients from the meat in it. It is solid at room temperature (a
creamy white/beige color) and when warmed up is thick and creamy and smooth.

Coconut Oil on the other hand has no fiber. The oil has been separated from the meat of the coconut.
It is solid at room temperature and translucent. If you see it solid, it may look like it has florets or
snowflakes in it. When warmed up, it becomes liquid and completely clear. It can be used for higher
temperature cooking such as sautéeing or frying.

HOW DO YOU USE ARTISANA COCONUT BUTTER?

Coconut butter can be spread on toast, added to smoothies, curries, cakes, cookies, drizzled over fresh
fruit or to make Pina Coladas. You do not want to use it to sautée anything. The solids will scorch.

HOW DO YOU USE ARTISANA COCONUT OIL?

You can use coconut oil the same way you would use a dairy butter or other vegetable oil. Spread on
toast, use it to sautée veggies, or use it in place of vegetable oil or shortening when baking.

Read our post about using our Coconut Oil and our Coconut Butter

THE COCONUT BUTTER JAR SAYS TO GENTLY WARM THE JAR TO
MAKE IT SPREADABLE. HOW DO I DO THAT?

There are several ways to warm up your jar of coconut butter. You can partially submerge the jar in
warm to hot water. If you have a microwave, you can put it in the microwave for 10 seconds at a time
(please be aware that the fibers in the coconut butter will scorch very easily). Or, you can wrap the jar
in a heating pad and let it melt slowly. You can even leave it in the warm sunshine for the afternoon!
Stirring will help it soften faster.

Whatever option you choose, once the jar is warm all the way thru it will be easily spreadable.
Depending on the temperature of the room it has been known to stay soft for several days.

I JUST BOUGHT SOME COCONUT BUTTER AND I OPENED IT UP AND
SAW WHITESNOWFLAKES SHAPE ON TOP.IS THIS MOLD?

Your Coconut Butter is just fine. This is not mold. Coconuts are roughly 60% oil, and that oil likes to
separate from the fiber in the Coconut Butter. When you first open your Coconut Butter, you may
find either a complete layer of translucent oil at the top or spots that look like snowflakes or florets.
You may also see these white spots around the rest of the jar. This is perfectly normal. All you need to
do is gently warm the jar and stir the oil back into the butter.

CAN I USE YOUR COCONUT BUTTER FOR SAUTEEING?

Coconut oil can be used in high heat cooking, but if you heat coconut butter up too much, the solids
will scorch or burn.

HOW DO YOU USE ALL OF THESE DIFFERENT BUTTERS?

Anything you can do with a peanut butter you can do with our butters. Make a nut butter sandwich,
use in cookies, smoothies or even make a peanut-free Pad Thai dressing. Check out our Instagram

WHAT MAKE ARTISANA SO AMAZING?

We are peanut-free, gluten-free, soy-free & dairy-free. We are certified Organic, Kosher, Non-GMO,
CLEAN and RAW. We do not add any extra oils, sugar, emulsifiers or preservatives to our pure raw

IS IT POSSILBLE THAT YOUR NUTS COOULS HAVE BEEN CROSS
CONTAMINATED WITH PEANUTS BEFORE YOU RECIEVED THEM?

We take our statement of being 100% peanut-free very seriously. All of our nuts and seeds are

WHAT IS THE SELF LIFE YOUR NUT BUTTERS?

The shelf life of our nut butters is 12 to 18 months from the date of manufacture, depending on the
item. You should be able to find a "Best By" date printed around the base of the jar, or on a sticker
applied to the bottom of the jar.

SHOULD I REFRIGERATE YOUR NUT BUTTERS AFTER OPENING?

here is really no need to refrigerate. Our butters are shelf stable. In fact if you chill them too much
they will not spread easily.

HOW DO YOU KEEP YOUR PRODUCTS FROM SPOILING? AFTER
PUTTING YOUR PRODUCTS IN JARS DO YOU HAVE TO HEAT THE JARS?

We do not heat the jars. Right after the product is processed we put it in jars to prevent exposure and
contamination when it is at its most sensitive point. We do not add anything to prolong the life of the
product.

HOW DO YOU SEAL YOUR LIDS FOR SAFETY?

The lids are machine tightened and then a safety seal is added that is under the label. The jars are
unable to be opened without breaking the safety seal. Some people expect to hear a "pop" when a jar
is opened, as this is common to many other nut butters. However, you will not hear a "pop" on ours,
because the "pop" comes from a heat-sealing process and we do not use heat to seal our jars.

ARTISANA PECAN AND WALNUT BUTTERS ARE BLENDED WITH
CASHEWS. WHY IS THIS? HOW MUCH OF THE BUTTER IS CASHEWS?

There are a few reasons we put cashews into these two highly acclaimed organic, raw nut butters.
Cashews make the walnut and pecan butters more creamy and spreadable. Cashews also act as a
natural sweetener to temper the natural bitterness of the walnut and pecan skins (which we leave in
for the health benefit). If you look at the ingredient list you'll see that pecans make up the majority of
ingredients in the Pecan Butter, and walnuts make up the majority of ingredients in the Walnut Butter.

HOW ARE YOUR ALMONDS PASTEURIZED?

We use California organic almonds in our Almond Butter and thus our almonds are pasteurized by our
growers and suppliers. They have found a proprietary process that meets state requirements and keeps
the temperature low. As soon as the technology becomes available they are planning on transitioning
over to a cold pasteurization process. We additionally have certified all our butters, including almond
butter, RAW and CLEAN to ensure that they retain a high bio-availability of nutrients from tree to jar.

HOW MUCH POTASSIUM IS FOUND IN ALMOND BUTTER?

On average, there are 113 mg (milligrams) of potassium in 16 grams of almonds (1 tablespoon). Keep
in mind, nutrient content may vary between brands, as many also add oils, sugars, or salts. Artisana
Almond Butter is 100% organic almonds, with no other added ingredients.

We often refer people to the USDA'S Food Composition Database to find nutrition facts on various
